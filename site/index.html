Owney

Your own mail server. Email, calendars, scheduling, and sign-in — one binary, one SQLite file, zero tracking.

curl -fsSL https://travisj.github.io/owney/install.sh | sh

Installs the latest build for Linux (x86_64, arm64) or macOS (Intel, Apple Silicon), verified by sha256. Or grab a tarball directly.

Everything your domain needs

Modern email, real standards

JMAP (RFC 8621) with push, plus IMAP and SMTP with DKIM, spam filtering, and full-text search built in.

Calendars that federate

Share calendars with people on your server — or on someone else's. Cross-server sync with granular permissions.

Booking pages

Publish a public scheduling page from your own domain. Visitors pick a slot; both sides get a real calendar invite. No third party.

Sign in with Owney

A built-in OpenID Connect provider with passkey login, so your apps can authenticate against the identity you already run.

Private by default

Encrypted storage, PGP support, no telemetry, and full S3 backups with point-in-time recovery. Your data stays yours.

One binary, one file

owneyd serves everything; SQLite stores everything. Setup wizard, health doctor, and safe self-update included.

Up and running in three commands

  1. curl -fsSL https://travisj.github.io/owney/install.sh | sh

    Install the latest build for your platform.

  2. owneyd setup

    The first-run wizard creates your config, generates keys, prints the DNS records for your domain, and can wait until they verify.

  3. owneyd serve

    Serve mail, calendars, the web UI, and booking pages. Run owneyd doctor anytime to check DNS, TLS, and deliverability.
